Obverse Design: State Capitol & Liberty Goddess

The 2026 design features the Texas State Capitol — the tallest in the nation and an enduring symbol of strength, governance, and the proud legacy of a state once its own republic. Anchored in rich architectural detail, the Capitol connects the piece to Texas heritage, while the placement of the year “2026” provides a refined and balanced finish.

Seated beside the Capitol is a graceful depiction of Lady Liberty, thoughtfully rendered to embody the enduring ideals of freedom and aspiration. With elegant motion, she extends an arm outward, holding the Lone Star above the Capitol dome — symbolizing guidance, unity, and the independent spirit that defines Texas.

The reverse design further reinforces these themes through the official Coat of Arms of Texas. Centered by the Lone Star and encircled by live oak and olive branches, the design symbolizes strength, resilience, and peace. The iconic state motto, “FRIENDSHIP,” reflects Texas’ rich heritage, culture, and enduring spirit.

Together, these elements and the complete design pay tribute to Texas’ rich history, independent spirit, and enduring legacy as a symbol of strength, stability, unity, peace, and freedom.
