Real Gold. Texas-Issued. Currency Style.

The Modern Redback is a first-of-its-kind gold note — a collectible that looks and feels like currency, but contains actual 24-karat gold embedded within a proprietary, patent-pending format. Made in the USA, each note is issued under the authority of the Texas Bullion Depository, the nation's first state-administered precious metals depository.

The 5 Centigram Lonestar Gold Note pays tribute to one of Texas's most iconic symbols: the Longhorn Steer. Set against the vast, rugged West Texas landscape, the design captures the spirit of independence and resilience that defines the Lone Star State. The denomination — 5 — appears in bold, with the gold content prominently declared: Five Centigrams Pure 24K Gold.

The reverse carries the Texas Bullion Depository mark and serial number, affirming authenticity and gold content. Each note includes a unique AA-series serial number, making every piece individually identifiable.

What Makes a Modern Redback

Unlike traditional gold coins or bars, the Modern Redback presents gold in a currency-inspired format — the first of its kind issued by a U.S. state agency. The gold is real, the design is distinctly Texan, and the format is unlike anything else in the precious metals market. Each denomination in the series features a different iconic Texas landmark or symbol, making the complete collection a portrait of the Lone Star State.

The Complete Redback Series

The Modern Redback collection spans three denominations, each with its own Texas subject matter:

  • 5 Centigrams — Longhorn Steer · West Texas landscape
  • 20 Centigrams — Texas State Capitol · Austin, Texas
  • 100 Centigrams — Alamo Mission · San Antonio, Texas

Each 5 Centigram Texas Lonestar Gold Note contains 0.05 grams of 99.99% pure (24-karat) gold. The gold is embedded within the note using a proprietary, patent-pending process developed in the USA. Gold content and purity are stated on the face of every note and backed by the Texas Bullion Depository. Learn more about verification ›

The 5cg Lonestar Gold Note is available as a single note or in bundles of 100. Bundles are banded and packaged for easy storage and gifting. With 5 grams of total gold content, a bundle of 100 represents a meaningful accumulation of physical gold in a distinctive Texas format.

The Modern Redback is not legal tender and is not intended to circulate as currency. It is issued solely for its commemorative, collectible, and gold-content value. The currency-inspired format is a design choice that honors Texas's historical connection to the gold standard era, including the era of paper currency redeemable in gold.

The Modern Redback

A Texas Original

In the 1800s, Texas issued its own paper currency — notes redeemable in gold, backed by the Republic and later the State of Texas. The original "Redbacks" were among the first paper money in American history tied directly to a state's gold reserves.

The Modern Redback revives that tradition for a new era. Currency-style notes, issued by the Texas Bullion Depository, each containing real 24-karat gold — made in the USA with a proprietary, patent-pending process. A collector's piece. A Texas statement.
