Official Coinage

State of Texas Lone Star Coins

Precision-crafted gold and silver commemorative coins featuring iconic Texas imagery, produced to the highest standards as collectible works of art that also meet investment-grade bullion purity.

  • Bullion & Proof editions in both gold and silver weights.
  • .9999 fine purity, comparable to sovereign issues from the U.S. and Royal Mints.
  • An enduring reverse by master designer Joel Iskowitz, constant across every release.
Explore the Coins
A Constant Across Every Release

The Enduring Reverse

While obverse designs may change, the reverse of every Texas commemorative coin remains constant, serving as the defining hallmark of the series. At its center is a detailed interpretation of the Official Coat of Arms of Texas, a symbol that traces its origins to the Republic of Texas, re-imagined to suit the medium by famed artist and coin designer Joel Iskowitz.

The reverse design, among Joel's last ever completed, features the iconic five-pointed Lone Star, encircled by live oak and olive branches bound together. Encircling the design are inscriptions that reinforce the identity and heritage of the state, including “THE STATE OF TEXAS” and the simple, iconic state motto that reflects its culture and people: “FRIENDSHIP”.

This enduring reverse establishes continuity across all future releases — regardless of metal, size, or finish — and is intended to become a recognizable symbol of Texas coinage for generations to come.

Gold Bills of Texas

The Modern Redbacks

A contemporary tribute to one of the most distinctive forms of early Texas currency, the Republic of Texas redbacks issued in 1839 and 1840, reimagined with enduring value through gold.

  • Verifiable 24-karat gold deposited onto a durable polymer substrate.
  • Six denominations from 1 to 100 centigrams for accessible fractional ownership.
  • Uniquely Texan scenes, from the longhorn to the bluebonnet, on every bill.

Joel Iskowitz

Master Medallic Artist · U.S. Mint Designer

For more than three decades, Joel Iskowitz earned worldwide recognition for merging historical authenticity with timeless artistic expression, becoming one of the most celebrated designers in modern numismatic history. The 2026 State of Texas Lone Star carries his vision into a new chapter of Texas heritage.

  • U.S. Mint — Arizona & District of Columbia State Quarters
  • Reverse of the 2009 Lincoln Bicentennial cent
  • Medals, government artwork & stamps for 40+ nations

The 2026 Lone Star is among his final works — a lasting testament to his artistry.
